Individual Development Plan
“Plans are useless—Planning is everything” - Dwight D. Eisenhower Planning the next steps for your career is the most important thing you can do while you are in graduate school. This development plan have been created to supplement your current Individual Development Plans (IDPs). A full IDP encompasses your research, academic, career, and skill set development (resources here). While the name IDP uses the word individual, this process should not be done alone. While it is your plan, finding multiple people to talk with about your goals and resources available will lead to a richer and more useful plan. If you need someone to talk with about your plan, email best@emory.edu for a one-on-one meeting with a career coach.
  • The goal for this IDP is to help make progress on the foundational elements of career and professional development—which are self-awareness and career exploration.

Informational Interviewing
An informational interview is a great way to reach out to someone who works in an industry that you're interested in learning more about. Think of it as an informational conversation. The goal is to learn about the person's career path, the industry, and/or the corporate culture of a potential future workplace. In turn, the person can share his or her insights and make connections for you if possible. How do I set up an informational interview?
  • Cold Email
    • Use your LinkedIn network, members in professional organizations, or connections from friends of friends
    • "Hi, I'm currently a graduate student at ____. I am studying ____ and am really interested in ____. I am exploring non-academic career options and I'd love to hear more about what you do. If you are not too busy, I hope we can find a time to chat, via phone or in person."
  • 20-minute phone call or a 30-minute meeting is a reasonable request
  • Mention how you found them
    • If connected through someone you know- mention it!
  • Keep the email short
  • Email subject is important
    • Include who you are & what you do
      • Emory PhD student requesting information
      • Request for career informational interview
      • Meeting request: Referred by [mutual acquaintance] for career advice
Elevator Pitches
A BRIEF way to introduce who you are and your professional interests
  • 10-60 seconds
  • Useful for networking at meet and greets, LinkedIn Bio, and talking to people outside of your field
  • Highlight your skills, confidence, goal, and motivation
  • 30 second pitch:
    • Should mention your title, company, and what you do
    • Use transferable skills related to what you want to be doing
    • How does your job help people?
    • What value do you bring?
    • Example: Experienced clinical research investigator with subject matter expertise in psychiatry and neurological disorders. I am driven in increasing the impact of new scientific discoveries to improve patient outcomes. I am most interested in using my scientific expertise to collaborate with healthcare professionals, scientists and non-scientists to increase access to the latest advances in science and medicine.
  • 60 second pitch:
    • What is your mission as a young professional?
    • What are you passionate about?
    • What’s the kind of work that doesn’t feel like work because you enjoy doing it so much?
    • How do you want to make an impact?

Your Online Presence
Using LinkedIn is to your advantage. Use it build and maintain your professional network. According to forbes.com, 98% of recruiters and 85% of hiring managers use LinkedIn to find candidates. LinkedIn makes it easier to follow companies and industries and find others with similar career interests.
  • Create relevant profiles
    • Don't set up accounts/platforms you won't use
  • Network Intentionally and Immerse Yourself
    • Follow companies you are targeting
    • Consider joining industry or alumni specific forums
  • Be Known as a resource
    • Be thoughtful, share, comment, be active
  • Keep professional exchanges off social sites
  • Do a little at a time, not all at once!
  • Follow companies' social media platforms, not just job search sites
