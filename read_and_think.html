Individual Development Plan
Before you begin looking for jobs, first it is important to assess your skills and the values that will be most important to you in a future career. A great way to gain this self-awareness is by filling out an Individual Development Plan (IDP). An IDP helps you break down long-term goals (that sometimes feel overwhelming) into more manageable tasks. A full IDP encompasses goals for your research, academic, career, and skill set development. While the name IDP uses the word individual, this process should not be done alone. Finding multiple people to talk with about your goals and resources available will lead to a richer and more useful plan. If you need someone to talk with about your plan, email best@emory.edu for a one-on-one meeting with a career coach.
The goal for the IDP below is to help make progress on the foundational elements of career and professional development—which are self-awareness and career exploration.

Career Exploration Resources

Find some inspiration for news ways to apply your PhD to various careers with the resources below.

  • TEDx Talk: Reimagining the PhD by Nadia Jaber
  • The NIH BEST Consortium Website: video interviews by Vanderbilt School of Medicine of alumni and their various career paths
  • The New York Academy of Sciences: Webinars and online courses about a variety of topics related to STEM careers
  • NIH Office of Intramural Training and Education: Archive of videocasts and lectures on career and professional development topics ranging from communication skills to career exploration to crafting your resume and CV
  • Science Careers: Large archive of past and current articles on a wide range of career options for PhD scientists and engineers
  • Versatile PhD: Online community and forum sharing info about alternative careers for PhDs
  • Personal Development Resources: A Google Doc compilation of books, articles, and videos on mindsets, diversity, self-awareness, etc. collated by Dr. Sharon L. Milgram, Director NIH OITE.
